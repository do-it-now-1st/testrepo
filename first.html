🇳🇵 Amazing Nepal

The land of the Himalayas, ancient temples, and incredible wildlife. Nepal is truly one of the most unique countries in the world!

🏔 The Himalayas

Nepal has 8 of the world's 14 highest mountains. No other country comes close to this!

🏔

Mount Everest

The highest mountain on Earth. Known in Nepali as "Sagarmatha" which means "Forehead of the Sky".

8,849m
⛰

Annapurna Circuit

One of the most famous trekking routes in the world. It goes through forests, villages, and deserts all in one trip!

230 km
🌨

Kanchenjunga

The 3rd highest mountain in the world. Local people consider it sacred and for many years did not allow anyone to reach the very top.

8,586m
🧗

Sherpa People

The Sherpa people live in the high mountains. Their bodies are naturally built for high altitudes — they can breathe easier in thin air than most people.

~150,000

🌿 Nature and Wildlife

Nepal goes from tropical jungle to Arctic snow in just 200 km. You can see rhinos and snow leopards in the same country!

🦏 One-Horned Rhino

Chitwan National Park has one of the biggest populations of the rare one-horned rhino in the world. Over 700 rhinos live there!

🐆 Snow Leopard

The snow leopard is very rare and shy. Nepal's high mountains are one of the only places you can find them. People call them the "ghost cat."

🐼 Red Panda

Eastern Nepal's forests are home to the cute red panda. It looks like a mix between a fox and a bear!

🐦 900+ Bird Types

Nepal has over 900 types of birds! That is more than the entire continent of North America. Bird watchers love visiting Nepal.

🌸 Rhododendron

Nepal's national flower is the rhododendron. In spring, whole hillsides turn red, pink, and white with these beautiful flowers.

💧 Phewa Lake

This beautiful lake in Pokhara reflects the Fishtail Mountain on its surface. On a calm morning it looks like a perfect mirror.

🏛 Culture and Heritage

"Nepal has over 125 different ethnic groups and 123 languages — all in one small country!"
  • 1.

    Pashupatinath Temple

    One of the most important Hindu temples in the world. It sits on the Bagmati River in Kathmandu and has been active for over 1,500 years without stopping.

  • 2.

    The Living Goddess (Kumari)

    Nepal has a very special tradition — a young girl is chosen from the Newar community and worshipped as a living goddess. There are separate Kumaris in Kathmandu, Bhaktapur, and Patan.

  • 3.

    Old Newari Buildings

    The temples in Kathmandu Valley (like Bhaktapur Durbar Square) are UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The wood carvings and brickwork are hundreds of years old and still standing!

  • 4.

    Birthplace of Buddha

    Lumbini, in southern Nepal, is where the Buddha was born. Millions of Buddhists from all around the world visit this place every year.

  • 5.

    Lots of Festivals!

    Nepal celebrates more festivals than almost any other country. Dashain, Tihar, Holi, Indra Jatra, Losar — almost every month has at least one big festival!

🤔 Fun Facts About Nepal

🚩

Unique Flag Shape

Nepal is the ONLY country in the world with a flag that is not a rectangle. It is two triangles stacked on top of each other!

🕐

Odd Time Zone

Nepal's time zone is UTC+5:45. That extra 45 minutes is very unusual — most countries use whole hours. Nepal wanted to be different from India and China!

📅

Different Calendar

Nepal uses its own calendar called Bikram Sambat. It is about 56 years ahead of the regular calendar. When the world celebrated 2024, Nepal was celebrating 2080!

🐄

Cow is National Animal

Nepal is the only country where the cow is the national animal. Cows walk freely on the streets and harming one is against the law.

🗺

Tiny but Tall

Nepal is a small country with no sea access, but it has the biggest height range on Earth — from 70m flat land all the way up to 8,849m on Everest!

🛡

Never Colonised

While most of Asia was controlled by European countries, Nepal was never colonised. It stayed independent by being very smart in its politics.
